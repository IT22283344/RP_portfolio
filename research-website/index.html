About the Project

Purpose and sustainability impact

Smart Fisher Lanka combines AI, environmental intelligence, and practical guidance to support safe, sustainable, and profitable coastal fishing decisions in Sri Lanka.

Purpose

Sri Lanka's coastal fishers regularly face uncertain weather, variable fish availability, and market fluctuations. This project delivers an AI-powered support platform that turns complex data into clear recommendations for daily operations.

AI + Sustainability Focus

The research promotes sustainable coastal practices by reducing unnecessary fuel usage, improving fish handling quality, increasing route safety, and helping crews make evidence-based decisions with confidence.

Target Users

Coastal fishers, vessel captains, fisheries coordinators, and local support teams who need reliable decision guidance before heading to sea.

Research Outcome

A validated prototype that demonstrates practical value through predictions, alerts, and easy-to-read dashboards tailored to the fishing context.

Expected Impact

Safer trips, better fuel efficiency, improved catch strategy, and stronger awareness of environmental conditions around the coastline.

Core System Components

Four intelligent modules

The platform is built around four connected modules that cover quality, location, trip safety, and market insight.

Fish Quality Grading

Uses AI-based image classification to assess fish quality grades quickly and consistently, supporting better post-harvest value.

Fish Zone Prediction

Combines weather and ocean-condition signals to identify favorable fishing zones and improve catch opportunity planning.

Fish Boat Trip Planning

Supports safe route optimization with sea-risk awareness, helping crews choose safer departure windows and travel paths.

Fish Price Prediction

Predicts market price trends using historical sales patterns, enabling better selling strategy and income planning.

Technologies Used

AI, web, and deployment foundation

The system combines lightweight web technologies with efficient AI models suitable for real-world decision support scenarios.

AI Models

MobileNet and TensorFlow Lite are used for efficient image classification and edge-friendly inference workflows.

Web Technologies

HTML, CSS, and JavaScript provide a responsive, maintainable, and presentation-ready interface for project demonstrations.

Backend and Data Layer

Python-based services and model APIs can support forecasting, data processing, and integration with weather or market feeds.
