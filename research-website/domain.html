Research Domain

AI support for sustainable coastal fishing

This project sits at the intersection of marine safety, environmental intelligence, and practical AI. The goal is to turn complex coastal data into understandable decisions for fishing communities in Sri Lanka.

Marine Forecasting Decision Support Sustainable Livelihoods
Smart Fisher Lanka focuses on practical outcomes: better trip planning, safer departures, and more sustainable catch decisions by integrating AI with real fishing conditions.

Literature Survey

Recent developments in Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Machine Learning (ML) have significantly improved decision-making in the fisheries sector. Modern systems use environmental data such as sea surface temperature, chlorophyll levels, and ocean currents to predict fish-rich zones more accurately than traditional methods. AI-driven heatmaps and ocean data analysis help identify high-probability fishing areas, reducing search time and fuel usage.

Machine learning techniques are also used for fish price prediction by analyzing historical trends, seasonal patterns, and market conditions. In addition, deep learning models such as Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) are applied for fish classification and quality grading using image data. Hybrid approaches are further used to estimate trip cost and fuel consumption based on operational and environmental factors. However, most existing solutions focus on individual tasks rather than combining all these functionalities into a single system.

Research Gap

Most existing fisheries tools provide only partial solutions, such as fish zone prediction or market analysis. These systems often present complex data that is difficult for fishermen to interpret and use effectively in real-world situations.

There is a lack of integrated, user-friendly systems that combine fish location prediction, price forecasting, quality grading, and trip cost estimation into one platform for practical decision-making.

Research Problem

Fishermen often make decisions without accurate and complete information, which leads to wasted fuel, inefficient trips, and reduced income. The absence of reliable tools for predicting fish zones, market prices, quality, and trip costs increases uncertainty in fishing operations.

The main problem is how to provide accurate, real-time, and easy-to-understand recommendations that support efficient and safe fishing decisions.

Research Objectives

The objective of this research is to develop an AI-based decision support system to improve fishing efficiency and reduce operational risks.

  • Predict fish-rich zones using environmental data
  • Forecast fish market prices using machine learning
  • Automate fish quality grading using deep learning
  • Estimate trip cost and fuel consumption
  • Provide a simple and user-friendly platform

Methodology

The system integrates multiple models for different functions. Environmental data is used for fish zone prediction, historical data for price prediction, image data for quality grading, and operational data for cost estimation.

Machine learning, deep learning, and hybrid models are combined into a unified system to generate real-time predictions and support decision-making.

Technologies Used

The frontend is developed using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript to create an interactive user interface. The backend uses Node.js and APIs for data processing and integration.

Machine learning and deep learning models are implemented using Python, TensorFlow, and Scikit-learn, with data processing performed using Pandas and NumPy.
