Research Domain

AI support for sustainable coastal fishing

This project sits at the intersection of marine safety, environmental intelligence, and practical AI. The goal is to turn complex coastal data into understandable decisions for fishing communities in Sri Lanka.

Marine Forecasting Decision Support Sustainable Livelihoods
Smart Fisher Lanka focuses on practical outcomes: better trip planning, safer departures, and more sustainable catch decisions by integrating AI with real fishing conditions.

Literature Survey

Existing coastal decision support studies show that weather forecasting, vessel tracking, and machine learning can reduce uncertainty. However, many solutions are built for broader maritime use and do not reflect the day-to-day constraints faced by Sri Lankan fishing crews.

This research reviews academic work on marine forecasting, fish availability prediction, safety systems, and AI-based advisory platforms to identify practical methods that can be adapted for local use.

Research Gap

Most available tools either provide raw marine data or focus on isolated functions such as weather reports. They rarely combine catch guidance, safety alerts, and user-friendly explanations in a single localized platform.

Smart Fisher Lanka addresses this gap by blending multiple signals into one decision workflow designed for fishermen, not just analysts.

Research Problem

Fishermen often leave shore with incomplete situational awareness, which increases the risk of unsafe trips, wasted fuel, and lower catch efficiency.

The problem is how to provide timely, accurate, and easy-to-understand AI recommendations that support safer and more sustainable fishing decisions.

Research Objectives

Build a decision support prototype that identifies favourable fishing windows, warns about dangerous conditions, and summarizes relevant insights in a simple interface.

Evaluate the system using sample weather, catch, and fishing-travel scenarios to demonstrate usefulness, clarity, and reliability.

Methodology

The research follows a structured flow: problem analysis, data understanding, prototype design, model integration, interface development, and iterative validation.

Feedback from academic review and testing is used to refine the recommendations, layout, and messaging so the system remains practical in a real setting.

Technologies Used

The website uses HTML, CSS, and JavaScript for the presentation layer. The research model layer is designed to work with Python, TensorFlow, data visualization libraries, and connected marine data sources.

Future deployment can extend to cloud hosting, API services, and IoT-connected inputs for richer coastal intelligence.
